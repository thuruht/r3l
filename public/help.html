rocket_launch Getting Started

What is R3L:F?

R3L:F (Relational Ephemeral Filenet) is a decentralized, ephemeral, anti-algorithmic social file-sharing platform that prioritizes user control, organic discovery, and community-driven archiving. Unlike traditional social media, R3L:F is designed to be non-corporate, minimalist, and free of algorithmic engagement farming.

Key differentiators:
  • Anti-algorithmic: No engagement optimization or content ranking
  • Ephemeral by default: Content expires after 7 days unless explicitly preserved
  • User-controlled: You decide what to share and how long it stays visible
  • Privacy-focused: Uses privacy-respecting technologies throughout
  • Community-driven archiving: Important content can be preserved through explicit voting

How to Join R3L:F

  1. Visit the home page and click "Get Started" or "Create Account"
  2. Choose a username and secure password
  3. Complete your account setup by creating a recovery key
  4. Customize your "drawer" (personal profile) and communique
  5. Start uploading and sharing content

Basic Navigation

The main navigation bar provides access to all R3L:F features:

  • Home: Return to the main landing page
  • Network: Explore the association web visualization
  • Map: Geographic visualization of content and users
  • Search: Find content using various criteria
  • Random: Discover random communiques
  • Upload: Share new content
  • Drawer: Your personal archive and public profile
  • Inbox: Access notifications and messages
  • Profile: Manage your account settings

account_circle Account Management

Creating Your Account

R3L:F uses a secure authentication system with JWT tokens. To create an account:

  1. Visit the registration page
  2. Choose a username (this will be your public identifier)
  3. Create a strong password
  4. Generate a recovery key (important for account recovery)
Important: Store your recovery key in a safe place. This is your only method to recover your account if you forget your password. R3L:F does not store your password and cannot reset it for you.

Managing Your Profile

Your profile page contains personal settings that control how you appear and interact within R3L:F:

  • Display Name: How you appear to others (can be different from your username)
  • Avatar: Upload a custom profile image or choose from defaults
  • Bio: A brief description visible on your profile
  • Visibility Settings: Control your presence in the association web
  • Lurker Mode: Toggle low-visibility status
  • Location Sharing: Control whether your content appears on the map
  • Change Password: Update your account security

Account Security Best Practices

  • Use a strong, unique password that you don't use elsewhere
  • Store your recovery key in a secure location (password manager or physical backup)
  • Regularly review your account activity
  • Be mindful of what information you share in your profile and communique
  • Log out when using shared devices

Customizing Your Avatar

Your avatar is your visual identity in R3L:F and appears in your drawer, communique, and the navigation bar:

  1. Go to your Profile page
  2. In the Avatar section, click "Change Avatar"
  3. Upload an image (JPG, PNG, or SVG recommended)
  4. Crop and adjust as needed
  5. Click "Save" to update your avatar

Your avatar will appear in your drawer, communique, navigation bar, messages, and anywhere your profile is referenced.

If you don't upload a custom avatar, a default icon will be used instead.

Ways to Preserve Content

  1. Personal Archive: You can add content to your personal drawer to preserve it privately
  2. Community Archiving: Content that receives enough votes from the community can be preserved in the public archive
  3. Last Communique: Non-archived content is appended to your last communique before deletion, creating a record of what was shared

Archive Voting System

The community archiving system works as follows:

  • Each user receives a limited number of archive votes daily
  • Content that exceeds a threshold of votes is eligible for community archiving
  • The content creator can flag high-vote content for permanent community storage
  • Archived content remains searchable and accessible to all users
Pro Tip: Use your daily votes strategically to help preserve valuable content that benefits the community. The limited vote allocation prevents gaming the system and ensures that only truly valuable content is preserved.

archive Drawer & Communique

Understanding Your Drawer

Your "drawer" is both your personal archive and public profile. It has two primary components:

  1. Personal Archive: Your private collection of saved content
  2. Public Communique: A customizable, public-facing presentation of your identity and selected content

Creating and Customizing Your Communique

The communique is your public-facing profile that others see when they visit your drawer. You can customize it to express your identity:

  1. Visit your drawer page and click "Edit Communique"
  2. Use the rich text editor to add text, links, and basic formatting
  3. Insert HTML and CSS for more advanced customization (all code is sanitized for security)
  4. Embed uploaded content by copying the embed code from your archive
  5. Save your changes to publish your communique
Communique HTML/CSS Guidelines:

You can use most HTML tags and CSS properties in your communique, including:

  • Text formatting: headings, paragraphs, lists, bold, italic, etc.
  • Links to internal and external resources
  • Images and media (embedded from your archive or external sources)
  • Basic CSS for layout, colors, fonts, and animations
  • Embedding content from your personal archive

All HTML is sanitized to remove potentially harmful elements like scripts, iframes from untrusted sources, and certain event handlers.

Managing Your Personal Archive

Your personal archive allows you to save and organize content:

  • Add content to your archive by clicking "Add to Drawer" on any content
  • Organize content with notes and tags
  • Control which items are visible in your public communique
  • Remove items from your archive when no longer needed
Pro Tip: Unconfigured drawers only show an anonymous avatar & display name and cannot join the association web. Take time to customize your communique to fully participate in the Rel.

hub Association Web

What is the Association Web?

The Association Web is a visual representation of the connections between users in R3L:F. It's automatically generated from public contact lists and shows how users are related to each other through degrees of connection.

Key features of the Association Web:
  • Automatically generated from public contact lists
  • Shows branching structure of connections with degrees of separation
  • Stronger links appear for frequent interactions
  • Clicking a node opens that user's profile
  • Low-visibility users appear muted with no clear associations

Navigating the Association Web

To explore the Association Web:

  1. Click "Network" in the main navigation
  2. The visualization shows users as circles, content as squares, and tags as diamonds
  3. Hover over nodes to see details
  4. Click on user nodes to visit their drawer/communique
  5. Use the zoom and pan controls to navigate the web
  6. Filter the view using the control panel on the left

Public vs. Private Contacts

In R3L:F, contacts can be designated as either public or private:

  • Public contacts appear in the association web, creating visible connections
  • Private contacts remain hidden from the association web
  • All mutual relationships must be explicitly agreed upon by both parties
Note: The Association Web only shows connections that both users have agreed to make public. There are no unwanted labels or connections - all relationships must be explicitly agreed upon.

visibility_off Lurker in the Mist

What is "Lurker in the Mist" Mode?

"Lurker in the Mist" is a low-visibility status option for users who prefer minimal presence in the R3L:F ecosystem. When enabled, this mode affects how you appear to others and how you discover content.

Effects of Lurker Mode:
  • Your presence in the Association Web is muted and connections are obscured
  • Your content appears with randomization in search results
  • Your profile appears with minimal information in listings
  • You receive modified recommendations based on broader patterns
  • Your activities have reduced visibility to other users

Enabling Lurker Mode

To enable "Lurker in the Mist" mode:

  1. Go to your Profile page
  2. Find the "Visibility Settings" section
  3. Toggle the "Lurker in the Mist" option
  4. Adjust additional visibility preferences if desired
  5. Save your changes
Privacy Note: Lurker mode provides additional privacy but is not complete anonymity. Your content and interactions are still identifiable to users who directly connect with you. For maximum privacy, consider using minimal personal information in your profile and communique.

  • Everything is searchable but not algorithmically promoted

notifications Inbox & Notifications

The Inbox System

R3L:F uses a centralized inbox for all notifications and messages, with no intrusive pop-ups. Your inbox contains:

  • Direct messages from other users
  • System notifications about your content
  • Expiration alerts for content about to be deleted
  • Connection requests and suggestions
  • Community archive voting notifications

Direct Messaging

The direct messaging system allows private communication between users:

  1. Start a conversation by clicking "Message" on another user's drawer
  2. Send text messages and file attachments
  3. All messages are secured with end-to-end encryption
  4. Conversations are organized in your inbox

Mutual Connection Prompts

R3L:F may suggest mutual connections based on interaction patterns:

  • Frequent viewing of the same user's content
  • Repeated engagement with the same group or files
  • Co-archiving 5 or 10 shared files

These suggestions appear in your inbox and require explicit confirmation from both parties to establish a connection.

Content Expiration Notifications

R3L:F notifies you about content that's about to expire:

  • Initial notification when content is marked for deletion (24 hours before deletion)
  • Final notification in the last hour before permanent deletion
  • Options to archive personally, nominate for community archiving, or let expire
Important: Content deletion is permanent after the 24-hour grace period. Make sure to check your notifications regularly to avoid losing content you want to keep.

security Privacy & Security

Privacy-First Approach

R3L:F is built with privacy as a fundamental principle:

  • No tracking of user behavior for advertising or recommendation algorithms
  • No collection of unnecessary personal data
  • No third-party analytics or tracking scripts
  • Bunny Fonts instead of Google Fonts
  • JWT-based authentication with secure HttpOnly cookies

Security Features

R3L:F implements several security measures to protect your data:

  • End-to-end encryption for direct messages
  • HTML sanitization for user-generated content
  • Secure authentication with password hashing
  • Recovery key system for account recovery
  • Content access controls based on user preferences

Control Over Your Data

R3L:F gives you control over your information:

  • Mutual Contributor Opt-Out: Hide your name from shared files
  • Permanent Hide List: Ignore specific connections indefinitely
  • Manual History Clearing: With multi-step confirmation
  • Content Lifecycle Control: Decide what expires and what stays
  • Visibility Settings: Control who sees your content and connections
Note on Anonymity: R3L:F supports pseudonymous users but not completely anonymous profiles. Unconfigured drawers remain limited (only showing a communique, no web presence).

psychology Philosophy & Motivation

Why R3L:F Exists

R3L:F was created as an alternative to traditional social media platforms, with a focus on:

  • User Agency: Giving people control over their digital presence
  • Digital Decay: Embracing the natural lifecycle of information
  • Intentional Preservation: Making archiving an active choice
  • Meaningful Connections: Focusing on quality over quantity
  • Privacy Respect: Building with privacy as a foundational principle

Anti-Algorithmic Stance

R3L:F deliberately rejects algorithmic content curation because:

  • Algorithms often prioritize engagement over user well-being
  • Recommendation systems create filter bubbles that limit discovery
  • Engagement optimization can promote divisive or sensationalist content
  • User agency is diminished when algorithms decide what you see
  • Organic, serendipitous discovery leads to more diverse experiences

Ephemerality as a Feature

The ephemeral nature of R3L:F is a deliberate design choice:

Content expires after 7 days by default because:

  • It reduces digital clutter and information overload
  • It encourages more authentic sharing without permanent records
  • It makes preservation an active, intentional choice
  • It mirrors the natural way information flows in real-world communities
  • It creates space for evolution and change over time

Community-Driven Values

R3L:F embodies several core values:

  • DIY Ethos: Encouraging creativity and personal expression
  • Community Curation: Collective decision-making about what's worth preserving
  • Non-Corporate Structure: Building outside the attention economy
  • Minimalist Interaction: Focusing on content rather than metrics
  • Intentional Connection: Creating relationships through mutual agreement

In the words of our community motto: "the rel is what you make it"

R3L:F exists outside of traditional social media structures. It is a social file-sharing network, not a clout-driven platform. Users interact through shared content, association, and voluntary archival rather than likes and followers.
